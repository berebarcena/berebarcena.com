Farmacias Benavides

PRODUCTIVITY ANALYSIS DASHBOARD

Project while working at Grupo Censere

Project Background

“Farmacias Benavides” is one of the biggest pharmacy shops in Mexico that sells a wide variety of products. Our task consisted in building an app for tablets which allowed managers for each of their stores to analyze and make a summary of their different productivity indicators. This would help them make easier and faster decisions and have a better control over their management. The project had a very high priority and we had a timeframe of 6 weeks to design and build the app. I was in charge of all the user experience, which also involved several meetings with the client.

The challenge

Being one of my first projects as part of the Censere team, I wasn’t very sure of where to start. The first thing I did was research different dashboards trying to accomplish a similar goal, to get a better understanding of how to organize all the information.

Given the time restriction we had, the Project Manager decided to use a frontend framework for the user interface

The work

I hand drew several sketches to explore different ways of organizing the data. This helped me do quick iterations with the team while validating and discarding several ideas.

EARLY SKETCHES AND PROTOTYPE

Once we defined the structure, I used Axure to build a prototype, allowing us to show the client our ideas and our planned flow for the app.

the results

The app allows managers to view all the information about their store and generate reports. They see a summarized quick view of the information and have options to extend it and see it in better detail. Red numbers represent negative data, helping the managers recognize places where things can be improved.

lessons learned

Being one of my very first projects as a UX designer, I was forced to grow and learn new skills. The number of restrictions were big, with such a short timeframe and a small team so this put me in a challenging position having to work quick and take different roles at the same time.
One of the biggest lessons was about interacting with the client and fully understanding requirements for them to be properly translated into usable designs.

ANDREA

E-COMMERCE SITE REDESIGN

Project while working at Grupo Censere

Project Background

Andrea is a company dedicated to selling shoes through catalogues across Mexico. They currently have an online presence, but it’s limited to sales people for them to get inventory online. Our job was to create a site with a brand new look and feel and responsive features, while sticking to their strict design guidelines. The project’s timeline was broken down into different phases and the first phase consisted of 10 months.

I was in charge of UX design as well as implementing parts of the front end. We had to work very closely with Andrea’s Marcom team as well as their own developers.

The challenge

The site has 3 different kinds of users, each one with access to different kinds of information and different features across the site. Another big challenge was that the end users for the site had little to no level of experience using the web. This meant that the designed needed to be extremely obvious to navigate through.

The work

I started by elaborating flow charts for the different kinds of users. This would help define what kind of information was needed for each and how to display it.

WIREFRAMES AND PROTOTYPE

We created low res wireframes in Balsamiq to get some feedback and better understand the client’s requirements, as we were working remotely with them and having a visual representation made it easier to transmit ideas.

After this, I built a fully functional prototype using Axure. This prototype showed every single one of the pages the real store was to consist of. This was extremely helpful as the company executives could use it to verify that all requirements and objectives were to be achieved by the site. This prototype also included several notes describing the intentional user behavior for every feature.

EARLY MOCKS

After several months of meetings, we got an approved prototype and started implementing it into the Magento store. We did a few design exercises in order to find the right visual style.

The results

In the end we built a fresh looking site keeping some features the client was not willing to let go. Unfortunately as an agency our research results and expertise in UX were not enough to convince the client to get rid of these features but we were able to make several changes to the sitemap and user flow to make it a lot easier for end users to reach their goal.

The big thing I learned from this project was how to work with a client that wanted to rebrand their site but was reluctant to change. This meant having to find the sweet spot between a happy client and the best user experience possible

Farmacias del ahorro

E-COMMERCE SITE REDESIGN

Project while working at Grupo Censere

Project Background

“Farmacias del Ahorro” is a popular pharmaceutical shop in Mexico. Just like “Farmacias Benavides”, they sell a wide variety of products. Our job was to make a redesign of their online store while maintaining their corporate identity. Our timeframe for the project was of about 8 weeks and the main objective was to get more people to register to the site.
I was in charge of the UX design and the frontend implementation.

the work

I started by analyzing the site’s usability weaknesses and found that one of the main problems was the overall registration process. The form was too long and complex, making it hard to digest even in a desktop

Irrelevant information

Cluttered complex form

I hand drew several sketches which helped me decide on a better way of organizing the information making sure it would be easier to digest. After a few iterations, I mocked it on Fireworks for the client to review and sign off.

We used an iterative process across all the redesigned areas through which every design was prototyped and signed off by the client.

THE RESULTS

The changes resulted in a 40% increase in registered users during the first 2 weeks as well as an increased overall traffic across the site.

Lessons learned

This project was a very challenging one because it didn’t only required me to use my existing knowledge but also to learn several different things. I was forced to learn some PHP and to use the basic Magento APIs to do the implementation. This also gave me a better understanding of the challenges a developer has when implementing a design, giving me a better understanding of capabilities and restrictions.

Designing a welcome back experience for a LinkedIn dormant user

This is a design challenge I did as part of an interview process with Linkedin

Why did the user leave in first place?
What to do in order to make him/her stay and never leave us again?
To gain a deeper understanding, I needed to talk with an actual dormant user

User Research

I spoke with some LinkedIn dormant users (luckily, a lot of my mexican friends are in this category) and found out some of the reasons that make them stop using it:

  • Cluttered, too many features
  • Laziness: it is way too complex to fill out the profile (Lack of motivation/ lack of interest)
  • Wide variety of information

On the other hand I asked them about what makes them stay engaged with an app (in this case with a social network) even after they reach their objective, and this is what I found:

  • Sense of belonging
  • Fear of missing something out
  • See what others are doing / curiosity
  • See relevant information depending on their profile
  • Personal or professional growth

How can we change people’s mindset regarding LinkedIn’s meaning?
How can we make them think “LinkedIn is the network to connect with people so you can achieve your goals” ?

User Persona

In order to gather my findings and thinking, I created the following persona which I’ll be refering to throughout the process to explain my design decisions.

Task Flow

Linkedin gave me the following goals:

  • Grow their network
  • Improve understanding of LinkedIn
  • Help them accomplish a task that they have come to LinkedIn for

So before skecthing, I needed to figure out the best way for my user to perform the task in order to achieve these goals. I started asking myself: At what point do I consider my users “hooked”?

Scenario

The ideas and wireframes as well as the final design are based on assumptions and a specific scenario:
User receives an email informing her that she has an invitation request from one of their colleagues. Since she is at the office, she opens the email in her browser.

Wireframes

I focused on making sure that the user knows, at first glance, something about the person who is adding her.

I tried to make the “welcome back” page the most interesting way possible for the user, so I proposed showing her people that I know she cares about, because they share the same interests (finance in this case). I’m assuming that this little ammount of information is taken from her profile.

Even though I let her connect with people, after the system detects that she is a “begginer profile user”, a message appears showing that her profile is lacking of content, “pushing her” to improve so she can look “pro” like the rest of the people in her field.

Visual styles

After some iterations, these are my final design propositions:

User gets an email notification

Welcome back page (after accepting request)

User adds people to her network

Notification about her incomplete profile

After the user decides to improve her profile, I take her to the Edit profile page.

There’s a small section that reads “Lacking inspiration for your profile?”, with this, I try to arise curiosity, so she can see what other people are doing right; stimulating the human feeling of competition, the one that make us become a better person, or in this case, a better professional.
